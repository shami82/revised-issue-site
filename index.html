The Situation

Bangladesh is one of the world's most densely populated countries, with approximately 170 million people living in close quarters. This high population density contributes to significant challenges in accessing basic resources.

About 24% of the population lives below the national poverty line, struggling to meet even their basic needs. Many rural districts, such as Barguna, Patuakhali, and Moulvibazar, experience extreme poverty, while urban areas like Dhaka and Chittagong face significant challenges, with many residents living in poor conditions.

The impact of poverty extends to food access, with approximately 25% of households considered food insecure, lacking reliable access to sufficient quantities of affordable, nutritious food. Alarmingly, around 36% of children under five suffer from stunting due to chronic malnutrition, and about 22% of children in this age group are classified as underweight, reflecting poor dietary intake and health.

Insufficient access to basic necessities such as food, clean water, and shelter continues to be a problem for millions, despite notable advancements in economic growth and poverty reduction in recent years. Many orphans and families are extremely vulnerable, facing daily hardships in finding the bare essentials for survival.

The situation is further intensified by frequent natural disasters, including floods and cyclones, which displace families and destroy critical infrastructure.

Our goal is to provide relief to those most in need, offering support and resources to families and orphans who cannot support themselves.
